# Acceptable Use Policy for Artificial Intelligence (AI)

**Policy Reference:** AUP-AI-001  
**Effective Date:** March 2026  
**Review Date:** March 2027  
**Policy Owner:** AI Governance Committee  
**Applies To:** All staff with access to AI-assisted decision systems  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of AI systems within the organisation. It establishes the responsibilities of all staff who interact with AI-assisted decision-making tools and ensures that AI is used in a manner consistent with our legal obligations, ethical commitments, and risk appetite.

This policy applies specifically to **High-Risk AI systems** as classified under the EU AI Act, including systems that affect individuals' access to financial services.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees who use AI systems to support decision-making
- All contractors and third parties with access to AI systems
- All AI systems classified as High-Risk under the EU AI Act
- All AI systems that provide recommendations affecting customers

---

## 3. Core Principles

### 3.1 AI Outputs Are Advisory Only

AI systems provide **recommendations**, not **decisions**. No AI output should be treated as a binding decision without human review and approval.

| AI Output | Human Action Required |
|---|---|
| Approval recommendation | Review and approve, reject, or escalate |
| Rejection recommendation | Review and approve, reject, or escalate |
| Risk score | Interpret in context; do not rely solely on score |
| Contributing factors | Consider alongside professional judgment |

### 3.2 Human Accountability

**You are accountable for every decision you make**, regardless of whether that decision follows an AI recommendation. The AI cannot be blamed for poor outcomes — accountability rests with the human decision-maker.

This means:
- You must understand the decision you are making
- You must be able to explain your decision if asked
- You must document your rationale for audit purposes

### 3.3 Professional Judgment

You are expected to exercise **independent professional judgment**. AI is a tool to support your decision-making, not a replacement for your expertise.

If you disagree with an AI recommendation:
- **Override it** — you have the authority and responsibility to do so
- **Document your reasoning** — explain why your judgment differs
- **Do not hesitate** — overrides are expected and encouraged

### 3.4 Transparency and Honesty

If a customer asks whether AI was used in their assessment, you must answer honestly. We are legally required to inform individuals that AI assists in decisions affecting them.

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses

The following uses of AI systems are **strictly prohibited**:

| Prohibited Use | Reason |
|---|---|
| Using AI as the sole decision-maker | Violates human oversight requirements |
| Rubber-stamping AI recommendations without review | Creates nominal, not meaningful, oversight |
| Batch-approving decisions based on AI output | Removes individual human accountability |
| Ignoring AI explanations when making decisions | Undermines transparency requirements |
| Sharing AI system access credentials | Compromises audit trail integrity |
| Using AI systems for purposes outside their approved scope | Violates governance approval conditions |

---

## 5. Required Actions

All staff using AI-assisted decision systems must:

### 5.1 Before Making a Decision

- [ ] Review the AI recommendation and risk score
- [ ] Review the contributing factors provided by the AI
- [ ] Consider whether the recommendation aligns with your professional judgment
- [ ] Consider any information the AI may not have access to

### 5.2 When Making a Decision

- [ ] Select your final decision (Approve / Enhanced Due Diligence / Reject)
- [ ] Document your rationale in the system
- [ ] If overriding the AI, explain why your judgment differs
- [ ] Confirm that you understand you are accountable for this decision

### 5.3 After Making a Decision

- [ ] If you suspect the AI made an error, report it to your team lead
- [ ] If you notice patterns of AI error, escalate to the AI Governance team
- [ ] Participate in feedback sessions when requested

---

## 6. Overrides

Overrides occur when your final decision differs from the AI recommendation. **Overrides are not penalised** — they are a sign of meaningful human oversight.

### 6.1 When to Override

You should consider overriding the AI recommendation when:

- You have information the AI does not have access to
- Your professional experience suggests a different risk profile
- The AI's contributing factors do not seem relevant to this case
- The AI recommendation seems inconsistent with similar past cases

### 6.2 How to Override

1. Select your final decision (which differs from the AI recommendation)
2. Document your rationale clearly
3. The system will automatically flag this as an override
4. No additional approval is required for routine overrides

### 6.3 Override Monitoring

Override rates are monitored to ensure:
- Oversight is meaningful (override rate not too low)
- AI is functioning correctly (override rate not too high)

If your personal override rate is significantly different from peers, you may be contacted for a coaching conversation. This is to understand your decision-making process, not to penalise you.

---

## 7. Reporting AI Errors

If you believe the AI has made an error — either a clearly incorrect recommendation or a pattern of poor recommendations — you have a duty to report it.

### 7.1 What to Report

- Single significant errors (e.g., high-risk case scored as low-risk)
- Patterns of errors (e.g., AI seems to underestimate risk for certain application types)
- Suspected bias (e.g., AI seems to recommend rejection more often for certain demographics)
- Unexplained changes in AI behaviour

### 7.2 How to Report

| Channel | Use For |
|---|---|
| Team Lead | Initial report of suspected error |
| AI Governance inbox | Pattern concerns or bias concerns |
| Incident reporting system | Significant errors that have caused harm |

### 7.3 Whistleblower Protection

If you report a suspected AI error in good faith, you are protected under our whistleblower policy. You will not face retaliation for raising concerns about AI system performance or ethics.

---

## 8. Training Requirements

All staff with access to AI-assisted decision systems must complete:

| Training | Frequency | Delivery |
|---|---|---|
| AI Acceptable Use Policy | On access grant | E-learning |
| Responsible AI Awareness | Annual | E-learning |
| System-specific training | On system change | Instructor-led or e-learning |

Training completion is tracked. Access will be revoked if mandatory training is not completed.

---

## 9. Compliance and Enforcement

### 9.1 Monitoring

The following are monitored for compliance:

| Metric | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Decision rationale quality | Ensure meaningful documentation |
| Override rate | Ensure meaningful oversight |
| Time-to-decision | Ensure adequate review time |
| Training completion | Ensure competence |

### 9.2 Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

| Severity | Example | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Minor | Incomplete rationale documentation | Coaching |
| Moderate | Pattern of rubber-stamping | Formal warning |
| Severe | Using AI as sole decision-maker | Disciplinary action |
| Critical | Deliberately circumventing controls | Termination |

---

## 10. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually, or sooner if:

- Regulatory guidance changes
- AI systems are significantly updated
- Incidents reveal policy gaps
- Feedback suggests improvements
